# Branding & Naming

## Name Candidates

### Tier 1: Strong Recommendations

| Name | Domain Ideas | Rationale | Risk |
|------|-------------|-----------|------|
| **SellCraft** | sellcraft.ai, sellcraft.in | "Craft" implies skill + quality. Professional. Global-ready. | Slightly generic |
| **CatalogIQ** | catalogiq.ai, catalogiq.in | "IQ" = intelligence. Positions as smart, not just generative. | Might feel enterprise-only |
| **BazaarMind** | bazaarmind.ai, bazaarmind.in | "Bazaar" = Indian roots. "Mind" = intelligence. Memorable. | Could feel too cutesy |
| **ListIQ** | listiq.ai, listiq.in | Short, clear, implies listing intelligence. Easy to spell. | Narrow (just listings) |

### Tier 2: Strong Alternatives

| Name | Domain Ideas | Rationale | Risk |
|------|-------------|-----------|------|
| **SellerPilot** | sellerpilot.ai | Autopilot for sellers. Implies automation. | Common naming pattern |
| **MerchAI** | merchai.in | Short, punchy, merchant + AI. | Pronunciation ambiguity |
| **CatalogOS** | catalogos.ai | "OS" = operating system narrative. | Overpromises at MVP stage |
| **SellSense** | sellsense.ai | Making sense of selling. Data-driven feel. | Forgettable |
| **ListGenius** | listgenius.ai | Clear value prop in the name. | Slightly cheesy |

### Tier 3: India-Rooted (Niche Appeal)

| Name | Domain Ideas | Rationale | Risk |
|------|-------------|-----------|------|
| **VyapaarAI** | vyapaarai.com | "Vyapaar" = business in Hindi. Deep Indian roots. | Non-Hindi speakers may not connect |
| **DukaanBrain** | dukaanbrain.ai | "Dukaan" = shop. Fun, memorable. | Conflicts with Dukaan app brand |
| **BechoAI** | becho.ai | "Becho" = sell (Hindi). Catchy, action-oriented. | Too informal for enterprise |

### Recommended Name: **SellCraft AI**

**Why SellCraft:**
1. Universally understandable — works for Tier 1 and Tier 3 sellers
2. "Craft" implies quality and skill — not just automation
3. "Sell" makes the value prop instantly clear
4. Works globally if we expand beyond India later
5. Easy to spell, pronounce, and remember
6. Good domain availability potential
7. Doesn't box us into "listings only" — selling encompasses our full vision

**Tagline options:**
- "Craft listings that sell."
- "AI-crafted listings for Indian sellers."
- "Your listings, intelligently crafted."
- "Sell smarter, not harder."

## Positioning Angles

### Angle 1: The Intelligence Angle
**For:** Investors, D2C brands, agencies
**Message:** "We don't generate content. We generate marketplace intelligence — and the content is just the output."
**Why it works:** Positions us as a data company, not a tool company. Higher perceived value and defensibility.

### Angle 2: The Speed Angle
**For:** Individual sellers, quick commerce brands
**Message:** "From product details to marketplace-ready listing in 10 seconds."
**Why it works:** Sellers value speed above all. Competing with the 2-hour manual process.

### Angle 3: The Revenue Angle
**For:** All sellers
**Message:** "Sellers using SellCraft see X% higher conversions on average."
**Why it works:** Direct ROI story. Indian sellers are outcome-driven.

### Angle 4: The India-First Angle
**For:** Patriotic sellers, Tier 2/3 market, media
**Message:** "Built in India, for India. The only AI that understands Indian commerce."
**Why it works:** Anti-Western-tool positioning. Trust signal. Pride factor.

### Angle 5: The Democratization Angle
**For:** Social impact narrative, PR, government partnerships
**Message:** "Giving every Indian seller the same listing intelligence that top brands use."
**Why it works:** Narrative for media coverage, government partnerships, ONDC alignment.

## Category Creation

### The Category: "Seller Intelligence"

**Why create a new category:**
- "AI writing tool" = crowded, commoditized, low perceived value
- "Listing optimization tool" = feature, not a company
- "Seller Intelligence Platform" = new category, we define the rules

**Category narrative:**
"Until now, marketplace success depended on experience, intuition, and expensive agencies. Seller Intelligence is the new category of AI-powered platforms that give every seller the same marketplace optimization capability that top brands have — powered by data, not guesswork."

**How to establish the category:**
1. Use "Seller Intelligence" in all marketing copy, PR, investor decks
2. Publish the "Indian Seller Intelligence Report" (quarterly benchmark study)
3. Create the "Listing Quality Index" (industry benchmark score)
4. Host "Seller Intelligence Summit" (virtual conference, quarterly)
5. Get press coverage defining the category
6. Ensure analysts and marketplace platforms use the term

## Visual Identity Direction

### Design Principles
- **Clean and professional** — not playful or startup-y. Sellers are business people.
- **Trustworthy** — muted colors, clear typography. Indian SMBs are skeptical of flashy tools.
- **Mobile-optimized** — everything should look great on a phone screen
- **Speed-conveying** — design should feel fast, efficient, modern

### Color Palette Direction
- **Primary:** Deep blue or teal (trust, intelligence, professionalism)
- **Accent:** Warm orange or amber (energy, India-association, action)
- **Background:** White/light gray (clean, fast-loading)
- **Avoid:** Red (aggressive), neon colors (untrustworthy), too many colors (confusing)

### Typography Direction
- Clean sans-serif for UI (Inter, Poppins, or similar)
- Easy to read at small sizes (mobile-first)
- Support for Devanagari script (Hindi support)

### Logo Direction
- Simple wordmark or wordmark + icon
- Icon should suggest intelligence/optimization (arrow up, brain, sparkle)
- Must work as favicon and app icon
- Must be legible at small sizes (WhatsApp profile photo, marketplace app icon)

## Content Voice & Tone

### Voice Attributes
- **Knowledgeable** — we know Indian marketplaces deeply
- **Direct** — no fluff, no jargon, clear value
- **Confident** — we know this works, and the data proves it
- **Approachable** — not intimidating, any seller can use this
- **Practical** — focused on results, not theory

### Tone Guidelines
- Avoid corporate-speak ("leverage", "synergize", "paradigm")
- Avoid excessive enthusiasm ("AMAZING!", "INCREDIBLE!")
- Use numbers and data whenever possible
- Hinglish is OK in social media and WhatsApp, not in formal marketing
- Address sellers as "you", not "our users" or "sellers"
